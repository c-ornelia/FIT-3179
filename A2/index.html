Pokémon Go Overview

Pokémon Go is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game released in 2016 that allows players to catch, train, and battle Pokémon in real-world locations using their smartphones. Since its launch, the game quickly gained massive popularity, reaching millions of downloads and active users worldwide. Over the years, both downloads and user numbers have fluctuated, with the highest activity in 2016 at 260 million downloads and 232 million users, and gradually stabilizing in later years, reflecting a loyal but smaller player base by 2023.

Regional Distribution

From 2016 to 2024, Pokémon Go maintained strong interest in Malaysia, with Pulau Pinang consistently at 100. Urban states like Selangor and Kuala Lumpur saw growing engagement, while Kelantan and Terengganu remained low. Overall, the game has a loyal fanbase, with certain regions showing consistently high activity.

Base Stats Analysis

The strongest Pokémon tend to be legendaries, like Mewtwo, Rayquaza, and Dialga, with extreme stats in Attack, Defense, or Stamina. Some Pokémon specialize in offense, such as Dragon, Fighting, and Psychic types, while others, like Steel and Rock types, are defensive tanks with high Defense and Stamina. Balanced Pokémon, such as Garchomp, Dragonite, and Metagross, perform well in multiple roles, combining decent Attack, Defense, and Stamina. Overall, survivability often outweighs raw Attack in battles, making bulky or well-rounded Pokémon highly effective.
