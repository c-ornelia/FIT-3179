Pokémon GO:
An Overview Behind “Gotta Catch Them All”

Overview

Pokémon GO, released in 2016, is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game where players catch and battle Pokémon in real-world locations. It became a global phenomenon at launch but soon lost about 70% of its players. Over time, it evolved from a viral trend into a stable game supported by a smaller, loyal fan base.

From 2016 to 2024, Pokémon Go remained highly popular in Pulau Pinang, maintaining an interest level of 100, likely due to its many PokéStops, Gyms, and active player community.

In contrast, urban states like Selangor and Kuala Lumpur showed fluctuating interest, while Kelantan and Terengganu stayed consistently low, and Sabah and Sarawak experienced a slight decline over time.

Analysis

Deoxys has the highest Base Attack, while Shuckle leads in Defense but also has the lowest Attack. Blissey tops Stamina with its massive HP.

Defender score is calculated by multiplying a Pokémon’s Stamina and Defense. Blissey rank in the S Tier as the toughest Gym defenders, while A+ Tier Pokémon offer strong bulk performance. A and B Tiers feature solid alternatives for most players, whereas C Tier Pokémon are less effective in Gyms and better suited for Raids or PvP battles.

S Tier Pokémon, led by Mega Rayquaza, offer the highest value for Stardust and Candy investments (used to power up and evolve Pokémon), demonstrating exceptional performance in Raids. A and B Tier Pokémon remain strong and reliable choices, while lower tiers are better suited for newer players or specific battle situations.

Player Preferences

Catching Pokémon remains the primary activity, emphasizing the game’s core focus on collection, while Stardust is most desired, reflecting players’ priority on powering up.
