Pokémon GO:
An Overview Behind “Gotta Catch Them All”

Introduction

Pokémon GO, released in 2016, is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game where players catch and battle Pokémon in real-world locations. It became a global phenomenon at launch but soon lost about 70% of its players. Over time, it evolved from a viral trend into a stable game supported by a smaller, loyal fan base.

To support its massive launch, the game required a strategic investment of over $500,000, primarily for the crucial server infrastructure needed to handle the immense initial player surge.

The game proved exceptionally profitable, earning over $550 million in its first year and peaking at over $908 million in 2020, with lifetime earnings far exceeding its initial development cost.

Regional interest in Pokémon GO tends to cluster around densely populated areas, where players have easier access to PokéStops and Gyms.

Urban regions show stronger engagement, while rural areas see less activity, reflecting how accessibility shapes the game’s popularity.

Who Are the Top Performers by Base Stat?

Deoxys has the highest Base Attack, while Shuckle leads in Defense but also has the lowest Attack. Blissey tops Stamina with its massive HP.

Mastering Battles with Top Attackers

Knowing the best attackers helps players win Raids, Gyms, and PvP battles more efficiently. They deal higher damage, defeat bosses faster, and counter tough defenders. Investing Stardust and Rare Candy in Top Tier Pokémons ensures better performance.

The Importance of Strong Defenders

Strong gym defenders help hold gyms longer, maximizing rewards and preventing other players from taking your spot. Pokémon like Blissey and Chansey are top choices due to their high HP and defense, making them time-consuming to defeat.

Players' Preferences

Catching Pokémon continues to be the most common activity, highlighting the game’s focus on collection. The strong demand for Stardust shows players’ emphasis on strengthening their Pokémon.
