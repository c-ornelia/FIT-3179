Pokémon GO:
An Overview Behind “Gotta Catch Them All”

Introduction

Pokémon GO, released in 2016, is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game where players catch and battle Pokémon in real-world locations. It became a global phenomenon at launch but soon lost about 70% of its players. Over time, it evolved from a viral trend into a stable game supported by a smaller, loyal fan base.

To support its massive launch, the game required a strategic investment of over $500,000, primarily for the crucial server infrastructure needed to handle the immense initial player surge.

The game proved exceptionally profitable, earning over $550 million in its first year and peaking at over $908 million in 2020, showing the game's significant financial success.

Beyond its global success, Pokémon GO’s popularity varies across regions. In Malaysia, regional interest concentrates in densely populated areas, where players have greater access to PokéStops and Gyms.

Urban regions show stronger engagement due to larger communities. In contrast, rural areas see less activity, reflecting how population density and accesibility influence the game’s overall popularity.

Who Are the Top Performers by Base Stat?

Having explored regional trends, we now focus on the core of Pokémon GO — the Pokémon themselves.

Base stats determine a Pokémon’s battle potential by defining its strengths and weaknesses.

Deoxys (Psychic) has the highest attack, Shuckle (Bug) ranks highest in defense but lowest in attack, and Blissey (Normal) leads in stamina.

Mastering Battles with Top Attackers

Having explained base stats, we can now explore which Pokémon excel in battle.

Knowing the best attackers helps players win Raids, Gyms, and PvP battles more efficiently. They deal higher damage, defeat bosses faster, and counter tough defenders. Investing Stardust and Rare Candy in Top Tier Pokémons ensures better performance.

The Importance of Strong Defenders

While strong attackers are key to winning battles, powerful defenders are just as important.

Strong gym defenders help hold gyms longer, maximizing rewards and preventing other players from taking your spot, as their high HP and defense make them time-consuming to defeat.

Players' Preferences

Beyond battles, it is also important to know what keeps players engaged.

Players prefer catching Pokémon, reflecting the game’s core focus on collection. The strong demand for Stardust also highlights their need to strengthen their Pokémon.
