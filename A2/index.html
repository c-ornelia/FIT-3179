Overview

Pokémon Go is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game released in 2016 where players catch and battle Pokémon in real-world locations. The game saw huge popularity at launch, with millions trying it out, but Pokémon Go lost about 70 percent of its player base. Over time, its player base became mainly composed of loyal fans who continue playing regularly, while new downloads slowed. This shift marks its transition from a viral trend to a game sustained by a smaller, dedicated community.

Regional Distribution

From 2016 to 2024, Pokémon Go remained highly popular in Pulau Pinang, maintaining an interest level of 100, likely due to its many PokéStops, Gyms, and active player community. In contrast, urban states like Selangor and Kuala Lumpur showed fluctuating interest, while Kelantan and Terengganu stayed consistently low, and Sabah and Sarawak experienced a slight decline over time.

Analysis

Deoxys has the highest Base Attack, followed by Zacian and Xurkitree. Shuckle, on the other hand, stands out for its exceptionally high Base Defense while also having the lowest Base Attack. Lugia, Regice, and Registeel also rank among the top defenders. Blissey and Chansey lead in Base Stamina with their enormous HP, making them some of the toughest Pokémon to defeat in Gyms, whereas Shedinja sits at the opposite extreme with only 1 HP.

S Tier Pokémon, led by Mega Rayquaza, offers the highest value for Stardust and Candy investments, demonstrating exceptional performance in Raids. A and B Tier Pokémon remain strong and dependable choices, while C and D Tiers serve as practical options for newer players . F Tier Pokémon are generally less effective in Raids but can still perform adequately in certain Trainer Battles.

The best Gym Defenders are Pokémon with high bulk, which is calculated by multiplying Pokémon Stamina and Defense. Blissey and Chansey rank in the S Tier as the toughest Gym defenders, while A+ Tier Pokémon offer strong bulk and reliable performance. A and B Tiers feature solid alternatives for most players, whereas C Tier Pokémon are less effective in Gyms and are better suited for Raids or PvP battles.
